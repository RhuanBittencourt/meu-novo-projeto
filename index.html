Alberta

Alberta is one of Canada's ten provinces.
With an estimated population of 4,067,175 in 2016, Alberta is the fourth most populous province in the country and the most populous of the three prairie provinces. Its area is approximately 660,000 square kilometers, which makes Alberta the sixth largest Canadian subdivision by land area. Alberta and neighboring Saskatchewan were formerly districts of the Northwest Territories until they were established as provinces on September 1, 1905.

Quebec

Quebec is the second most populous province in Canada, after Ontario.
It is the only one with a predominantly French-speaking population, with French being the only official language at provincial level. Most residents live in urban areas near the Saint Lawrence River, between Montreal and Quebec City, the capital. Approximately half of the province's residents live in Greater Montreal, including the Island of Montreal. British Columbia

British Columbia is one of the ten provinces of Canada.
British Columbia, a province located in the far west of Canada, is defined by its Pacific coastline and mountain ranges. Natural areas like Glacier National Park have hiking and biking trails, as well as camping areas. Whistler Blackcomb is a major ski resort that hosted the 2010 Winter Olympics. The Sea-to-Sky Highway connects Whistler with Vancouver, a city known for its film industry, on the province's southern border with the United States.
